Let's Learn About Canada

There are 10 provinces and 3 terriroies in Canada. The provinces are, in alphabetical order: Alberta, British Columbia, Manitoba, New Brunswick, Newfoundland and Labrador, Nova Scotia, Ontario, Prince Edward Island, Quebec, and Saskatchewan. The three territories are Northwest Territories, Nunavut, and Yukon.

The interactive map shows the different provinces and territories in Canada. If you zoom into the map, you are able to see the labels for each province and territory. To learn some fun facts about each, read below!

Nunavut

Nunavut was officially separated from the Northwest Territories in 1999. It has a Total area of 1,994,000 km2 and its capitol is Iqaluit. Nunavut’s floral emblem is the purple saxifrage and it's national animal is theThe Canadian Inuit dog, which is called “Qimmiq” in Inuktitut. Nunavuts offical bird is calledthe rock ptarmigan (Lagopus Mutus), which is called “Aqiggiq” in Inuktitut. It is truly an arctic bird, as it does not migrate and lives in Nunavut throughout the year.
