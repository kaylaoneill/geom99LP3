Let's Learn About Canada

There are 10 provinces and 3 territories in Canada. The provinces are, in alphabetical order: Alberta, British Columbia, Manitoba, New Brunswick, Newfoundland and Labrador, Nova Scotia, Ontario, Prince Edward Island, Quebec, and Saskatchewan. The three territories are Northwest Territories, Nunavut, and Yukon.

The interactive map shows the different provinces and territories in Canada. If you zoom into the map, you can see the labels for each province and territory. To learn some basic fun facts about each, read below!

Alberta

Alberta is the most populous of the three Prairie Provinces. Bordered by British Columbia to the west and Saskatchewan to the east, it is one of only two provinces that are landlocked. It has a total area of 661,190 km2 and its capital is Edmonton. Alberta's floral emblem is the wild rose, also known as the prickly rose, and its official bird is the great horned owl. Its official animal is the Rocky Mountain bighorn sheep and its official fish is the bull trout.

British Columbia

Located on the west coast of Canada, British Columbia became the sixth province of Canada when it joined Confederation in 1871. It has a total area of 947,800 km2 and its capital is Victoria. British Columbia's floral emblem is the Pacific dogwood, and its official bird is the Steller’s jay. Its official animal is the Kermode bear or “spirit bear”.

Manitoba

One of Canada’s Prairie Provinces, Manitoba’s southern and western regions are predominantly grassland. Its eastern and northern regions are dominated by Canadian Shield lakes and forests. A small area of Manitoba’s extreme north is arctic tundra. It has a total area of 649,950 km2 and its capital is Winnipeg. Manitoba's floral emblem is the prairie crocus, and its official bird is the great gray owl.

New Brunswick

One of Canada’s three Maritime Provinces, New Brunswick is constitutionally bilingual. While the majority of the population speaks English, there is also a large Francophone minority – mostly of Acadian origin. It has a total area of 73,440 km2 and its capital is Fredericton. New Brunswick's floral emblem is the purple violet, and its official bird is the black-capped chickadee.

Newfoundland and Labrador

Located in the country’s Atlantic Region, Newfoundland and Labrador is the most easterly province of Canada. It has a total area of 405,720 km2 and its capital is St. John's. Newfoundland and Labrador's floral emblem is the insect-eating pitcher plant. Its official animals are the Newfoundland dog and Newfoundland pony, and its official bird is the Atlantic puffin. The partridge or ptarmigan is the provincial game bird.

Nova Scotia

One of Canada’s three Atlantic Provinces, Nova Scotia is located almost exactly halfway between the Equator and the North Pole. It is the second-smallest province in Canada. It has a total area of 55,490 km2 and its capital is Halifax. Nova Scotia's floral emblem is the trailing arbutus or mayflower. Nova Scotia's official animal is the Nova Scotia duck tolling retriever dog, and its official bird is the osprey.

Ontario

Located in east-central Canada, Ontario has the biggest population in the country and is the second-largest province in total area. It has a total area of 1,068,580 km2, and its Capital is Toronto. Ontario is also home of Ottawa, the capital city of Canada. Ontario’s floral emblem is the white trillium, also known as the wake-robin. The official bird of Ontario is the common loon.

Prince Edward Island

One of Canada’s three Maritime Provinces, Prince Edward Island is the smallest province in both land area and population. It has a total area of 5,660 km2 and its capital is Charlottetown. Prince Edward Island’s floral emblem is the lady’s slipper, and its official bird is the blue jay. Prince Edward Island has adopted the Charlottetown soil series as its official soil.

Quebec

Located in east-central Canada, Quebec is the only Canadian province that has a predominantly French-speaking population, and the only one to have French as its sole provincial language.It has a total area of 1,650,680 km², and its capital is City of Québec. Quebec's floral emblem is the blue flag and its official bird is the snowy owl.

Saskatchewan

Saskatchewan is one of Canada’s three Prairie Provinces, Saskatchewan is bordered on the west by Alberta and on the east by Manitoba. It has a total area of 652,330 km2. Its capital is Regina. Saskatchewan’s floral emblem is the western red lily, and the white-tailed deer is its official animal. Saskatchewan also has an official bird: the sharp-tailed grouse and the walleye is its official fish.

Northwest Territories

The Northwest Territories are bordered by Canada’s two other territories – Nunavut to the east and Yukon to the west. Most of the Northwest Territories were known as the North-Western Territory until 1870. It has a total area of 1,171,918 km2 and its capital is Yellowknife. The Northwest Territories’ floral emblem is the mountain avens and its official bird is the gyrfalcon. It doesn't have an official animal, but it does have an official fish: The Arctic grayling.

Nunavut

Nunavut was officially separated from the Northwest Territories in 1999. It has a total area of 1,994,000 km2 and its capitol is Iqaluit. Nunavut’s floral emblem is the purple saxifrage, and its national animal is the Canadian Inuit dog, which is called “Qimmiq” in Inuktitut. Nunavut’s official bird is called the rock ptarmigan (Lagopus Mutus), which is called “Aqiggiq” in Inuktitut. It is truly an arctic bird, as it does not migrate and lives in Nunavut throughout the year.

Yukon

The westernmost and smallest of Canada’s three federal territories, Yukon was split from the Northwest Territories in 1898. It has a total area of 483,450 km2 and its capital is Whitehorse. Yukon’s floral emblem is the Fireweed, and its official bird is the raven. While the Yukon does not have an official animal or fish, its official tree is the subalpine fir.
