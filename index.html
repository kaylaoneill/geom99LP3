Let's Learn About Canada

There are 10 provinces and 3 territories in Canada. The provinces are, in alphabetical order: Alberta, British Columbia, Manitoba, New Brunswick, Newfoundland and Labrador, Nova Scotia, Ontario, Prince Edward Island, Quebec, and Saskatchewan. The three territories are Northwest Territories, Nunavut, and Yukon.

Prince Edward Island

One of Canada’s three Maritime Provinces, Prince Edward Island is the smallest province in both land area and population. It has a total area of 5,660 km2 and its capital is Charlottetown. Prince Edward Island’s floral emblem is the lady’s slipper, and its official bird is the blue jay. Prince Edward Island has adopted the Charlottetown soil series as its official soil.

Quebec

Located in east-central Canada, Quebec is the only Canadian province that has a predominantly French-speaking population, and the only one to have French as its sole provincial language.It has a total area of 1,650,680 km², and its capital is City of Québec. Quebec's floral emblem is the blue flag and its official bird is the snowy owl.

Saskatchewan

Saskatchewan is one of Canada’s three Prairie Provinces, Saskatchewan is bordered on the west by Alberta and on the east by Manitoba. It has a total area of 652,330 km2. Its capital is Regina. Saskatchewan’s floral emblem is the western red lily, and the white-tailed deer is its official animal. Saskatchewan also has an official bird: the sharp-tailed grouse and the walleye is its official fish.

Northwest Territories

The Northwest Territories are bordered by Canada’s two other territories – Nunavut to the east and Yukon to the west. Most of the Northwest Territories were known as the North-Western Territory until 1870. It has a total area of 1,171,918 km2 and its capital is Yellowknife. The Northwest Territories’ floral emblem is the mountain avens and its official bird is the gyrfalcon. It doesn't have an official animal, but it does have an official fish: The Arctic grayling.

Nunavut

Nunavut was officially separated from the Northwest Territories in 1999. It has a total area of 1,994,000 km2 and its capitol is Iqaluit. Nunavut’s floral emblem is the purple saxifrage, and its national animal is the Canadian Inuit dog, which is called “Qimmiq” in Inuktitut. Nunavut’s official bird is called the rock ptarmigan (Lagopus Mutus), which is called “Aqiggiq” in Inuktitut. It is truly an arctic bird, as it does not migrate and lives in Nunavut throughout the year.

Yukon

The westernmost and smallest of Canada’s three federal territories, Yukon was split from the Northwest Territories in 1898. It has a total area of 483,450 km2 and its capital is Whitehorse. Yukon’s floral emblem is the Fireweed, and its official bird is the raven. While the Yukon does not have an official animal or fish, its official tree is the subalpine fir.
