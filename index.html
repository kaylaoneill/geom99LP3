Let's Learn About Canada

There are 10 provinces and 3 terriroies in Canada. The provinces are, in alphabetical order: Alberta, British Columbia, Manitoba, New Brunswick, Newfoundland and Labrador, Nova Scotia, Ontario, Prince Edward Island, Quebec, and Saskatchewan. The three territories are Northwest Territories, Nunavut, and Yukon.

Northwest Territories

The Northwest Territories are bordered by Canada’s two other territories – Nunavut to the east and Yukon to the west. Most of the Northwest Territories were known as the North-Western Territory until 1870. It has a total area of 1,171,918 km2 and its capital is Yellowknife. The Northwest Territories’ floral emblem is the mountain avens and its offical bird is the gyrfalcon. It doesn't have a offical animal but it does have an offical fish:The Arctic grayling.

Nunavut

Nunavut was officially separated from the Northwest Territories in 1999. It has a total area of 1,994,000 km2 and its capitol is Iqaluit. Nunavut’s floral emblem is the purple saxifrage and it's national animal is theThe Canadian Inuit dog, which is called “Qimmiq” in Inuktitut. Nunavuts offical bird is calledthe rock ptarmigan (Lagopus Mutus), which is called “Aqiggiq” in Inuktitut. It is truly an arctic bird, as it does not migrate and lives in Nunavut throughout the year.

Yukon

The westernmost and smallest of Canada’s three federal territories, Yukon was split from the Northwest Territories in 1898. It has a total area of 483,450 km2 and its capital is Whitehorse. Yukons floral emblem is the Fireweed and its offical bird is the raven. While the Yukon does not have an offical animal or fish, its offical tree is the the subalpine fir.
